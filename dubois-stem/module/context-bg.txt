The motivation for creating the visuals designed and created by Du Bois and his collaborators was to tell, on a world stage, the state and progress of Black Americans 35 years after emancipation from slavery.  
\n
Thomas J Calloway, organized the “Exhibition of the American Negro” to be shown during the 1900 Paris Exposition, and commissioned Du Bois, his Fisk University classmate, to create a set charts, graphs, photographs and other artifacts for the world stage. 
\n
Du Bois, along with collaborators at Atlanta University spent four months creating the materials the exhibit.
